A book would be cool!

I waffle between two approaches: narrative and reference. They are not mutually exclusive by any means. A lot of the essays are great in their tight focus on a certain thing or scenario. I love that. I can reference what happened with you.

With narrative, something like Christina Wodtke’s OKR book was actually very compelling and enjoyable to read. She did include an index/nuts-n-bolts kinda section at the end.

I like both approaches for the different mindsets they serve: narrative is a fun way for a beginner to learn and understand this new space; reference is great for those more in the know but who haven’t experience everything.

Having lots of excellent content is a good problem to have. :-)
